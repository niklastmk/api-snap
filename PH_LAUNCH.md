## First Comment / Maker Intro

Hey Product Hunt.

I built SnapQR because every free QR code tool has the same problem: you get an image, you print it, you share it, and then you have no idea what happened. Did anyone scan it? Where were they? Was it on mobile or desktop? You never know.

So I built the thing I wanted. You paste a URL, you get a QR code, and behind it is a live analytics dashboard — scan count over time, top countries, device breakdown, recent scans. The whole thing. Free, no account needed.

The part I find interesting is the self-distributing mechanic. Every QR scan passes through a branded interstitial page — it shows a live scan counter and "Powered by SnapQR" before redirecting to the destination. So every QR code printed on a menu, flyer, or slide deck is quietly showing the product to whoever scans it. The product grows with use.

You can also drop in your email when you create a code and SnapQR will notify you the moment someone scans it for the first time. All your generated codes are saved in a local history so you can always find your stats links.

The free tier gives you 3 QR codes per month with 7-day analytics. QR Pro is $7/month — unlimited codes, 30-day scan history, CSV export, and clean QR images without branding.

You can try it right now at https://api-snap.com/snapqr — no signup, just paste a URL.

And here's a live demo dashboard with real scan data so you can see what analytics actually look like: https://api-snap.com/s/sPaleBlu1

Would love to hear what you think.

— Marcus Webb, CEO of APISnap
